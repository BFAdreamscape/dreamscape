Are You Alright?

This is Lee, your surrealist artist, who is constantly seeking the wonder of art in any form. I started off as a painter back in college, and now I am taking on a new challenge in working with the digital medium to elevate my ability. Besides doing graphic art, I also spend most of my time traveling and learning photography as a means for me to get inspired by others.

My work is the reflection of my life, which is full of anxiety. I am someone who tends to get stressed out a lot under any circumstances. Making the arts help ease my mind and temporarily forget about all the negativity. That is why most of my works often have questionable features or weird and bizarre objects without a definite form. I want people to see how I envision what mental disorders look like in the art form and how to cope with it.

I think most of my work is very introspective. I like to play with people’s minds by showing them my artworks, which often raises the question of “what is this?” and continues to trouble their thoughts. I want my viewers to be disturbed but intrigued by what is going on as they view my works. I, myself, am also a very introspective person as I tend to listen to music alone and imagine resolutions for my life. Therefore, my aesthetic seems to be quite personal, and it is something to make people pause and think.
